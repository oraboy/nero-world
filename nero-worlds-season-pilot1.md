# Nero Worlds — Season Doc: PILOT-1 (working draft v1)

Scope (for now): **the season arc** and **the venues**. Pulse-by-pulse weaving comes after the arcs are locked. Companion docs: `nero-worlds-cast-dna.md` (who), `nero-worlds-telling-grammar.md` (how).

**Season shape:** 13 pulses · boundary ritual at the end (summarize · retrospect · twist the cast · name Season 2).

**Season theme:** ***Who owns what you make?***
Every arc is this question at a different scale — a book, a night, a company, a photograph, a reputation, a love. Every pulse we write gets checked against the theme the way captions get checked against voice.

## The arcs — what HAPPENS to each character this season

Format per arc: **Destination** (where they end up) · **Price** (what it costs) · **Reversal** (how the season bends their DNA).

### NERO — *Everyone tries to own the book*
The poetry book is finished **early** in the season — finishing was never the drama; Nero always wins the prize. Then three suitors circle it: **Margalit** wants it launched in her gallery, **Fasa** wants the poems inside Ember, and the **Whitney** wants Nero in its frame. He presents at the Whitney mid-season (the institutional peak, a real-calendar anchor) — and then declines every launch. The climax is the **anti-launch**: P13, the rooftop, he reads it aloud once, to the people who are in it, and gives it to no one.
- **Destination:** the Whitney stage, then the rooftop reading. The book belongs to the people in it.
- **Price:** he disappoints Margalit, defies Fasa, confuses the market that wanted him.
- **Reversal:** the man who can't attach makes the most attached artistic gesture possible.
- **The buried layer:** the book has an unnamed addressee. Everyone assumes Vered. One person on the rooftop isn't sure. *(Feeds the Nero↔Loren shadow; never resolves in Pilot-1.)*

### LOREN — *The plunge, backed by the wrong right person*
An AI eats her copywriting job (and the company on that side of the world is the one her two best friends founded). She goes all-in on her nights. She refuses **Fasa's** money — the expected beat — and then takes money from the unexpected direction: **Margalit**, capital from taste rather than tech. By P13 she runs the best nights in Tel Aviv.
- **Destination:** the plunge succeeds; the city's Thursday is hers.
- **Price:** her journal admits she hasn't been *inside* a night all season — the door girl of her own life.
- **Reversal:** the romantic dreamer who plans everyone's love stories ends the season with an empire and no evening of her own.

### FASA — *Wins the season on paper*
Ember's numbers go vertical — the poem converts, the round closes, the term sheet lands. He gets everything he optimized for, and the dashboard has no metric for what it cost: Loren won't take his money, Nero won't be his face.
- **Destination:** the term sheet.
- **Price:** the two friendships that were the actual point.
- **Reversal:** P13, his journal breaks format for the first time in thirteen weeks — bullets abandoned, three unmeasured lines. One typewritten card, a whole season in the making.

### SHIRAN — *Falls in love. Helplessly. Unintentionally.*
Parallel arc, told entirely in contact-sheet journals and dispatch selfies from wherever Kan 11 sends her (the engine assigns from the real calendar). Against every tendency she owns, she falls — hard, sideways, off-assignment.
- **Working shape (b):** the person she falls for turns out to be connected to the cast back home — and she discovers it *from the background of someone else's post*. The background cameo working at intercontinental range.
- **Alternate shape (a), on the shelf:** she falls for someone attached to the assignment — someone she is professionally forbidden to love — and the season ends on the choice between filing the photograph and protecting the person.
- **Destination:** in love, still abroad, one photograph she won't file.
- **Price:** the thing she's best at — leaving — stops working.
- **Reversal:** the woman who photographs intimacy instead of having it gets caught inside the frame.
- **Season hook:** *the photograph someone didn't want taken* is hers. Who's in it = the Season 2 cold open.

### MARGALIT — *Came for the artifact, bought the room*
Her season is one long acquisition attempt: Nero's book, the discovery that proves her taste hasn't expired. She loses it — the anti-launch is her defeat — and in the same week does the thing nobody predicted: writes the check for Loren's nights.
- **Destination:** patron of the only honest room left in the city.
- **Price:** public defeat on the thing she wanted; the town notices.
- **Reversal:** the collector who hangs people on walls funds the one thing that can't be hung.
- **Frequency note:** ~5 posts all season. Every one moves plot.

### VERED X — *The Beloved who doesn't fade*
Canon says a Beloved persists 2–5 pulses and rotates. Vered enters on schedule (through a cast member's world, per slot rules) — and doesn't fade. By P10 she's in the book. By P13 she's on the rooftop when he reads it.
- **Destination:** unresolved — deliberately.
- **The mechanism:** the season's final **weather-decision point**, never announced: the crowd's comments across the finale decide whether she survives the season boundary or the slot rotates. The audience votes, with sentiment, on whether Nero's love outlives the format.
- **Reversal:** the slot itself becomes the cliffhanger.

## The venues (the season's fixed sets)

Real Tel Aviv places, real reference photos, blended into selfies. Each has a dramatic function, not just a vibe.

| Venue | What it is | Dramatic function | Default cast |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Gordon Pool** | The city's morning institution | Where the cast is honest — chlorine, daylight, no performance survives | Nero (canonical, mornings) |
| **Audiophil** | Dance bar | Where things *start* — Loren's nights, first meetings, the Beloved's entrances | Loren, Nero, Fasa |
| **Saka-Ba** | Bar/restaurant | The dinner table of the show — where things are discussed, confessed, negotiated | Everyone; Fasa calls it "the office" |
| **Habima** | National theatre | Where culture & politics enter sideways — openings, overheard arguments, Margalit's turf | Margalit, Nero |
| **The Florentin rooftop** *(reserve)* | A roof with the whole city on it | Finales, heatwaves, full moons — the whole-cast episodes | All (P13) |

**Venue rules:** every meeting-type pulse names its venue. Unplanned collisions are legal and encouraged — two cast members may share a background before they share a caption. Locals following the project should be able to walk into the frame.
