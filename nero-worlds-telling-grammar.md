# Nero Worlds — The Telling Grammar (v1.0 · LOCKED)

Sibling to `nero-worlds-season-pilot1.md` (what happens) and `nero-worlds-cast-dna.md` (who they are). This document defines *how the story is told*. Every story pitch must declare which moves it uses.

---

## 1 · The anatomy of a pulse (the "My Week" format)

One pulse = one week = one post per active feed. Each post is a carousel with a fixed anatomy:

| Card | Channel | Register | Job |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Card 1 — My Week** | Journal entry (typewriter typography) | **Private voice** | 2–3 lines of *what was cooking*. Interiority, doubt, the unsaid. |
| **Cards 2+ — Selfies** | 1–3 images | **The moment** | The week compressed into frames. Real venues blended in. |
| **Caption** | Text under the post | **Public voice** | How the character *performs* the week to their followers. |
| **Comments** | The crowd + other avatars | **Weather & dialogue** | The audience's editing room; the cast's public scenes. |

**The double delta.** Drama in this medium lives in gaps:
- **Between feeds** (cross-feed): two captions of the same night disagree.
- **Within a feed** (journal vs. caption): what a character admits privately vs. performs publicly.

The journal is the engine's weekly simulation summary made *diegetic* — the machine's account of the week, in the character's hand.

### Journal rules (locked)
1. **Typewriter. Simple.** One typographic language for all journals; the *form* of the writing differentiates the writer, not the font.
2. **English first.** May switch later; revisit at a season boundary, never mid-season.
3. **2–3 lines, hard cap.** A journal that runs long starts narrating; narration is the enemy.
4. **Raises questions, never resolves them.** "What was cooking," never "what happened."
5. **May contradict the caption.** Encouraged. That contradiction is a plot beat.
6. **Form is characterization:**

| Character | Journal form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **Nero** | Fragments — half poem, half diary. Never explains. | *"the pool at 7. her sentence at 11. / everything since is commentary."* |
| **Loren** | Timestamps and verdicts. | *"out by 1:14. told him no. / told myself maybe."* |
| **Fasa** | Bullets; metrics leaking feelings. | *"• shipped. • +40% WoW. / • slept badly, unrelated."* |
| **Shiran** | Contact-sheet captions — places, no feelings. | *"Gate 4. Rented car. Didn't call."* |
| **Margalit** | One sentence. Always exactly one. | *"The boy still thinks it was his idea."* |
| **Vered X** | Unfixed — her form is still forming. That's the point. | — |

## 2 · The eight telling moves

1. **The pulse post** — the canonical weekly beat, one per active feed, in the My Week anatomy. Everything else decorates this.
2. **The cross-feed cut** — same event on two-plus feeds with conflicting captions. The medium's version of editing. The audience triangulates the truth.
3. **The background cameo** — a character appears unnamed in someone else's frame before entering the story. Works at any range, including intercontinental.
4. **Comment dialogue** — avatars speaking to each other *publicly* under a post. The medium's version of a scene. Rare = powerful; a low-frequency character's single comment is an event.
5. **The visible silence** — a skipped week, an archived post, or (strongest form) a journal card with no selfie. Absence as a beat.
6. **Stories as B-roll** — mid-week ephemeral fragments, published as stories *and as reels archived in a season Highlight* (Pilot-1 Highlight). Ephemeral in the moment, canonical in the rear-view: late arrivals binge the Highlight like a "previously on." Nothing plot-critical may exist *only* here.
7. **The weather-decision point** — a designed fork where the engine reads the crowd's comments over 1–2 pulses and lets them tilt an outcome. Announced never, honored always.
8. **The Rashomon pulse** — the set-piece form of the cross-feed cut: one event, three-plus feeds, each caption claiming the evening. Once per season. It's the showstopper.

**The governing principle: the audience is the editing room.** Followers connect what no single feed states. Any story that requires a narrator has drifted from the medium.
